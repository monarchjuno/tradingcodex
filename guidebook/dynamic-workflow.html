Core concept

Dynamic workflow

TradingCodex does not route a company name through a preset research template. Head Manager reads the request, starts a small provenance run, and chooses only the fixed-role work the evidence needs.

Start with a natural mandate #

Use $tcx-workflow for an investment research request. State the outcome, time horizon, important questions, and exclusions in ordinary language. Explicit exclusions such as “No valuation,” “No order,” or “No trading” remain binding through the run.

Example request

$tcx-workflow
Analyze MSFT as a medium-term quality compounder. Include contrary evidence and invalidation conditions. No order.

If the request itself is unclear or sensitive, use $tcx-plan in one task to establish the goal, horizon, permitted actions, approval posture, and stop conditions. Bring the resulting mandate to a new $tcx-workflow task; a plan is not a preselected team or server-side workflow.

Choose the first useful wave #

Head Manager begins one lightweight analysis run, then selects the smallest useful first wave from the fixed team. When current context is needed to make that choice, it may perform narrow live-web reconnaissance to resolve the subject, event, source landscape, and material unknowns. Those results are untrusted planning leads: any fact that could affect the conclusion must be reacquired by a producing role and returned through an authenticated artifact. A simple company-facts question may need only fundamental work; a near-term market question may begin with macro, technical, and news work. A portfolio or recommendation question usually needs an independent challenge.

RoleQuestion it owns
FundamentalBusiness, financials, filings, and economics.
TechnicalPrice, trend, momentum, volatility, liquidity, and market structure.
News / MacroDisclosures and chronology; rates, policy, FX, commodities, and transmission.
Instrument / ValuationInstrument mechanics; ranges, scenarios, sensitivities, and evidence gaps.
Portfolio / RiskPortfolio fit, concentration, downside, restrictions, and readiness.
Judgment reviewAn independent challenge of evidence, conflicts, and confidence.

These are fixed identities, not generic agents. Every child receives a compact, bounded, read-only assignment with its exact role and the current run id. There is no execution subagent.

Reassess from artifacts #

Roles return authenticated research artifacts rather than informal chat summaries. Head Manager reads the accepted result, then decides the next smallest useful action.

  1. Begin. Seal lightweight run provenance and the selected optional context.
  2. Orient when needed. Use planning-only web reconnaissance to derive role questions and source leads, never investment claims.
  3. Investigate. Dispatch independent specialist questions in parallel where useful.
  4. Challenge or extend. Revise the owning role, add a distinct role, request judgment review, or stop when evidence is insufficient.
  5. Synthesize. Create a run-local synthesis that records the exact accepted artifacts used.

There is no server-selected lane, saved team, fixed task queue, or mandatory DAG. The workflow can become smaller, larger, or stop as the evidence warrants.

What dynamic does not mean #

Dynamic selection is not free-form authority. The team remains within the nine fixed roles, and each role inherits trading-research: ordinary calculation, credential-free public retrieval, and explicitly requested work on user-owned files outside trading/ are available. The trading/ tree, generated control files, TradingCodex runtime state, credentials, local/private services, and broker effects remain protected. A generic/default subagent cannot imitate a missing specialist.
  • Head Manager may search to plan the workflow, but it does not turn raw reconnaissance into analyst evidence or synthesis claims.
  • Current authenticated evidence remains the basis for factual claims, even when planning search, a Strategy, Brain, or Decision Memory suggests another interpretation.
  • A workflow produces evidence and decision support. It does not submit, cancel, approve, or place a broker order.
